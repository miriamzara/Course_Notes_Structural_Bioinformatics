\paragraph{How can you measure the quality of a structural alignment?}
here exist several metrics. Most common are the RMSD (Root Mean Squared Deviation)
and the TM-score.
RMSD simply measures the average square distance between corresponding aligned atoms
(typically C alpha). Lower RMSD thus indicate better alignment.
TM-score is also based on distances, but it is designed in a way that i) it is normalised with
respect to protein size and ii) it is less sensitive to local outliers.
A higher TM-score indicates better alignment. Compared to RMSD, TM-score better
captures the preservation of the overall global topology.
